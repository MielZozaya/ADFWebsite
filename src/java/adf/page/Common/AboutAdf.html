What is ADF

ADF stands for the ‘Alcohol, Drugs & the Family’ group. This is a group of clinicians and researchers which was officially established in 1980s, although some members have been working in this field since the late 1960s.

The group is focused on:

  • highlighting the impact alcohol or drug problems can have on the rest of the family,
  • developing ways to help family members,
  • and raising the profile of research and good practice in this area.

The group has been based in different University and National Health Service (NHS) centres over the years, but its core at present is as a two-centre collaboration between the cities of Birmingham and Bath , incorporating some of the NHS and academic institutions in the surrounding areas.

Two of the pioneer members: Richard Velleman and Jim Orford are still very actively involved; other key members of the group at the present time are Alex Copello and Lorna Templeton, many others have participated in and made significant contributions to the group’s research over the years.
